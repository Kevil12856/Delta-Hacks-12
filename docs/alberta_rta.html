Residential Tenancies Act - Alberta

Section 29: Termination for substantial breach by tenant

29(1) If a tenant commits a substantial breach of a residential tenancy agreement, the landlord may terminate the tenancy by serving the tenant with a notice at least 14 days before the day that the tenancy is to terminate.

(4) A notice must (a) be in writing, (b) be signed by the landlord, (c) state the reasons for the termination, and (d) set out the termination date.

Section 43: Security deposits

43(1) A landlord must not require a tenant to provide a security deposit that is greater than one month's rent under the residential tenancy agreement.

(2) A landlord who receives a security deposit must deposit it in an interest-bearing trust account representing the security deposit within 2 banking days after receiving it.

Section 23: Entry of premises

23(1) Except as otherwise permitted in this section, no landlord may enter the residential premises rented by the landlord without the consent of the tenant or of an adult person lawfully on the premises.

(2) A landlord is entitled to enter the residential premises without consent if the landlord has reasonable grounds to believe that (a) an emergency requires the landlord to enter the premises, or (b) the tenant has abandoned the premises.

(3) A landlord is entitled to enter the residential premises without consent but after notice to the tenant to (a) inspect the state of repair of the premises, (b) make repairs to the premises, (c) show the premises to prospective purchasers or mortgagees of the premises, or (d) show the premises to prospective tenants after a landlord or tenant has served notice of termination of a periodic tenancy or during the last month of a fixed term tenancy.

(4) A landlord must give the tenant at least 24 hours' written notice of entry.
